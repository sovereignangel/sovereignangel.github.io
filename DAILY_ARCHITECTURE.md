## 2. Workday Architecture

Every workday follows this structure. No exceptions unless energy mode is RECOVER.

### ~7:00am — Wake: Journal + Meditate (15-20min)
- **Why first:** Mind is fresh and uncluttered. Best for clarity, introspection, intention-setting.
- **Journal:** Free-form. What's alive? What matters today? What am I avoiding?
- **Meditate:** 10min minimum. Breath focus or open awareness.

### ~7:30-9:45am — Training Block (~2hr 15min)
- **Run from Homebrew to gym** (~30-45min, ~3 miles)
  - **Audible book** during run. Current: *Making Sense of Chaos* by Doyne Farmer.
  - As books are completed, queue the next from the intellectual roadmap.
- **Weight training** (~45min)
  - Mon: Push | Tue: Glutes | Wed: VO2 Max intervals | Thu: Pull | Fri: Glutes
- **Subway home** (~35-45min)
  - **Audible book** continued.
- **Factor meal** at home upon return.

**Audible Queue:**
1. *Making Sense of Chaos* — Doyne Farmer (IN PROGRESS)
2. TBD — queue next as current book approaches completion

### ~10:00am-12:00pm — Intense Study (2hrs)
- **Why post-exercise:** BDNF, prefrontal blood flow, and norepinephrine peak 30-60min after training. This is the highest-cognition window of the day.
- **Sources:**
  - **Intellectual roadmap:** Stanford RL (CS231n, CS224r), complexity economics papers, AI/ML textbooks (Sutton & Barto, Goodfellow, de Prado)
  - **Execution skills:** Whatever Armstrong or Alamo Bernal currently needs (quant methods, backtesting frameworks, specific tech)
- **Rule:** The study block is SACRED. No meetings, no Slack, no email. Deep analytical work only.

### ~12:00-5:00pm — Work Block (1-2 Theme Focus)
- **Maximum 2 themes per day.** Not 3, not 4. Deep and concentrated.
- **Which themes:** Determined by the day's allocation (see weekly rhythm below).
- **Alamo Bernal:** ONLY on designated Alamo days (default: Tue + Thu). Never on other days.
- **Armstrong:** Primary work theme on non-Alamo days.
- **Research/Other:** Can share a day with Armstrong but not with Alamo Bernal.

### ~5:00pm-7:00pm+ — Evening: Love & Play
- **Non-negotiable.** This is not spillover work time.
- **Activities:** Aidas, Homebrew community, AI Engineering Group meetups, creative projects, dinner with friends, aesthetic experiences.
- **Community evenings:** AI Socratic, EAIG, AGI Reading Group — these happen in this window.

### Before Bed — Journal Reflection
- Brief. What happened today? What am I grateful for? What would I do differently?
- This feeds into the morning brief's AI synthesis for tomorrow.

## 5. Planning Philosophy

### What We Don't Do
- **Eisenhower matrix / todo lists** — Too much cognitive overhead. Decision fatigue competes with deep work.
- **Granular task checkboxes** — 20-30 checkboxes per week is a clipboard, not a compass.
- **Scattered theme days** — Working on 3-4 themes in a day destroys flow.

### What We Do
- **One daily intention** with specific numbers and outcomes. Three lines: study, work, evening.
- **Theme-level weekly goals** with 2-4 measurable outcomes (not 6 sub-tasks each).
- **Weekly scorecard** — 8 metrics computed from daily logs. Multiplicative ruin if any hits zero.
- **AI-generated morning brief** — Compass, not clipboard. Adapts based on energy, journal, and momentum.

### Neuroscience Basis
- Todo lists consume working memory and create decision fatigue (Masicampo & Baumeister, 2011)
- A single clear intention reduces cognitive load and increases follow-through
- Post-exercise cognition is optimal for analytical work (Ratey, "Spark")
- Protected evening/play time prevents burnout and enables creative insight (default mode network)

## 6. Morning Brief Structure

The daily morning brief (delivered via Telegram at 9am ET) follows this structure:

1. **Energy Mode** — GO / CONSERVE / RECOVER (from Garmin data)
2. **Daily Intention:**
   - **Study (9-11am):** What to study and why. Specific chapter/paper/lecture.
   - **Work (11am-5pm):** Which 1-2 themes, with specific outcome and numbers.
   - **Evening (5-7pm+):** Love & play / community suggestion.
3. **Theme Context** — 1-2 sentences: why these themes today, what's the leverage.
4. **Signals** — Top 2-3 unread signals worth reading.
5. **Reconnect** — 1 person if someone is overdue for contact.
6. **Keynes Check** — Warm one-liner about love, beauty, or play.
7. **AI Synthesis** — 2-3 sentences connecting yesterday to today.

### What We Removed
- Todo counts and Eisenhower quadrants
- Job pipeline tracking (not job hunting)
- Stalled projects list (noise)
- "Top 3 Plays" (replaced by single daily intention)
- Discernment exercise (replaced by Keynes check)

|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Eisenhower matrix todos | Too much cognitive overhead, never engaged with | Single daily intention |
