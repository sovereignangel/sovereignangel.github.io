# Kiteboarding Mastery Plan — Palanga Sprint

**Window**: 2026-07-14 → 2026-09-23 (10 weeks, Palanga, Lithuania)
**Outcome A**: Fully independent rider — launch, ride upwind on both tacks, transition, self-rescue, return to your towel without walking. Non-negotiable.
**Outcome B**: Jumping rider with a Surfr logbook — consistent 2m+ jumps, 2s+ airtime, landed clean.
**Both outcomes share the same sessions — every hour on the water compounds toward both.**

## Background

- Current level: almost independent — water start and riding are there, upwind is not reliable
- Cadence assumption: 3-5 kiteable days/week, 2-3 hrs/session. Baltic reality: expect ~50-60% of days to have rideable wind (11+ kn). That is roughly 25-40 sessions and 60-100 hours on water over 10 weeks — more than enough for every milestone below.
- Tracking: Surfr app. Primary KPIs: **airtime, jump height, jump distance** (plus session count, distance ridden, upwind angle)
- Daily wind brief: Telegram notification at 07:30 (Kite Wind — Palanga) gives today's best 2-3h window and the week outlook. Plan the day around the window, not the other way.

## Target Skill: Independent Freeride + Early Airs

What "mastery in two months" actually means at your starting point:

- **Weeks 1-3**: upwind riding is the whole game. Nothing else matters until you can hold ground.
- **Weeks 4-5**: transitions and control — you stop being a passenger.
- **Weeks 6-8**: pop and first jumps — Surfr KPIs come alive here.
- **Weeks 9-10**: jump progression and consolidation — leave Palanga with numbers on the board.

The trap to avoid: chasing jumps before upwind is automatic. Every hour spent walking back up the beach is an hour not spent progressing. Upwind first, always.

## Strategy: Session Structure + No-Wind Protocol

**Every session (2-3 hrs)**:
1. First 20 min — warm-up riding, both tacks, no goals
2. Middle 60-90 min — the ONE focus drill of the current phase (below). One drill per session, repeated to boredom.
3. Last 30 min — free ride, consolidate, end on a success
4. After: log the session (Surfr auto-logs; add one line of notes — what clicked, what didn't)

**No-wind days (the 40-50%)**: these are not rest days, they are the "blog post" of this plan:
- Trainer kite or kite-flying on the beach (kite control is 70% of every skill)
- Video review: watch your GoPro/phone footage or 1-2 technique videos for the current phase drill
- Fitness: grip, core, legs — 30 min. Jumping punishes weak landings.
- Max 1 technique video topic per no-wind day. Watching 10 tutorials teaches nothing.

## 10-Week Sprint Plan

### Phase 1: Upwind Lock-In (Weeks 1-3, Jul 14 – Aug 2)

**Week 1 (Jul 14-20)** — Diagnose and Edge
- Focus drill: ride 100m holding a hard edge — back leg pushed, hips open to the kite, front shoulder pointing where you want to go, board tail biting
- Kite at 10-11 o'clock (not 12) while riding; park it, stop working it
- Milestone: end a session at most 100m downwind of where you started

**Week 2 (Jul 21-27)** — Both Tacks Equal
- Focus drill: 10 upwind legs per session, alternating tacks; your weak side gets double reps
- Look upwind at your target, not at the kite
- Milestone: hold ground on BOTH tacks; weak-side riding feels boring, not scary

**Week 3 (Jul 28 – Aug 2)** — Upwind Is Automatic
- Focus drill: "return to towel" — every single run ends where it started; zero walking all week
- Add: ride through gusts and lulls without losing the edge (Baltic wind is gusty — this is the home-field skill)
- **Gate milestone: 3 consecutive sessions with zero walk-of-shame. Do not enter Phase 2 without this.**

### Phase 2: Transitions & Control (Weeks 4-5, Aug 3 – Aug 16)

**Week 4 (Aug 3-9)** — Transitions
- Focus drill: sliding transitions — slow down, kite to 12, sink, redirect, power away on the new tack. 20+ per session.
- Milestone: 8/10 transitions without body-dragging or sinking out

**Week 5 (Aug 10-16)** — Toeside + Chop
- Focus drill: toeside riding 50m+ each direction; carving turns (heelside-to-toeside)
- Practice deliberate relaunch + self-rescue once this week in moderate wind (you want this automatic before jumping)
- Milestone: linked carving transition both directions; comfortable in chop at speed

### Phase 3: Pop & First Airs (Weeks 6-8, Aug 17 – Sep 6)

**Week 6 (Aug 17-23)** — Pop (board-only)
- Focus drill: edge hard, load the lines, pop off the water WITHOUT sending the kite — small board-off-water hops, 20+ per session
- This teaches the load-and-release timing that makes real jumps safe
- Milestone: clean pop with both feet leaving the water, landing riding away

**Week 7 (Aug 24-30)** — First Sent Jumps
- Focus drill: small sends — kite from 11 to 12:30, edge hard, let go of the edge, land with kite back to 11. Start underpowered.
- **Start the Surfr logbook this week: first logged airtime**
- Milestone: 5 landed jumps in one session; Surfr shows airtime > 1.0s

**Week 8 (Aug 31 – Sep 6)** — Jump Consistency
- Focus drill: 15+ jumps per session, focus on soft landings (kite redirect BEFORE landing, land tail-first, downwind)
- Milestone: **height 1.5-2m, airtime 1.5s+, 70% landed clean**

### Phase 4: Progression & Consolidation (Weeks 9-10, Sep 7 – Sep 23)

**Week 9 (Sep 7-13)** — Height
- Focus drill: harder edge + faster send + more speed on approach = height. September Baltic wind is stronger — use it.
- Add grabs once landing 80%+ (grabs force compact body position, which improves everything)
- Milestone: **Surfr height 2.5m+, airtime 2s+**

**Week 10 (Sep 14-23)** — Personal Bests + Exit Review
- Goal sessions: chase Surfr PBs on height, airtime, and distance in the best wind windows
- Final session: full self-audit ride — launch alone, ride upwind 1km, 10 transitions, 10 jumps, land at your towel
- Write the exit review (below) before flying to NYC

## Wind Reality Rules (Palanga)

- **11-14 kn**: light session — biggest kite, upwind drills only, no jump attempts
- **14-18 kn**: prime learning wind — drills + jumps, this is where progression happens
- **18-24 kn**: strong — smaller kite; great for jumps IF landings are already at 70%+, otherwise control drills
- **24+ kn or gusts 35+**: beach day unless you are ahead of schedule and honest about it
- **Any E wind (offshore)**: never ride. No exceptions. The next stop is Sweden.
- The 07:30 Telegram brief encodes these rules — trust its verdict, plan the day around the window it names

## Failure Modes to Avoid

- Skipping the Phase 1 gate because jumping looks fun (single biggest progression killer)
- Sending big jumps in gusty wind before landings are consistent (single biggest injury source)
- "Just cruising" sessions with no focus drill — fun, but they don't move KPIs
- Kiting through fatigue in hour 3 — injuries cluster in the last 30 tired minutes; end on a success
