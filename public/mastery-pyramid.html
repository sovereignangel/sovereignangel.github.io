Part I · The Model – 01

What Mastery Means Here

Mastery in this field is not erudition and it is not a portfolio of hobbies. It is the capacity to convert uncertainty into capital, repeatably, and to prove it. A master research-and-technology fund manager can take a vague intuition about how the world is changing, formalize it into a falsifiable hypothesis, build the instrument that tests it, survive the test financially and psychologically, and then raise money against the artifact that test produces – and do this on a cadence, alone if necessary, while the tools underneath them change every quarter.

Notice what that sentence contains: a theorist, an engineer, an investor, a risk officer, a fundraiser, and a contemplative – fused. The rarity is not any single skill. Plenty of people can backtest. Plenty can raise. The rarity is the integration, held by one mind, compounding into one track record. That is the apex of the pyramid, and it is also the thing the market will pay the most for and copy the least.

The Primary Threat

The dilettante's curse: many practices, no compounding artifact.

You already named this as your central long-term risk, and you are right to fear it more than any market regime. A person who studies DRL, complexity economics, options theory, and Mahamudra in parallel – and ships nothing that compounds – has a beautiful interior and an empty track record. The black-belt frame is the cure precisely because it is graded against demonstration. You cannot earn a belt by reading about the kata. You earn it by performing it, watched, under fatigue. Every belt in this book is defined by an artifact that survives contact with reality: a tearsheet, a deployed agent, a wired check, a published paper, a logged drawdown survived.

The black belt is a beginning, not an end

This is the single most misunderstood thing about the metaphor, and it is the most important thing for you. In traditional Japanese systems, shodan – first-degree black belt – literally means "beginning degree." It certifies that you have finally absorbed the fundamentals well enough to begin learning the art. The colored belts are remedial; the black belt is matriculation. Mastery – the fluency that looks like ease, the mushin of an operator who is no longer reacting – lives in the dan grades that follow, over years.

So the honest claim of this primer is narrow and achievable: twelve months of correct, graded practice takes you from white belt to first dan – to a credentialed, independent, fundamentally-sound operator with a complete track-record artifact and the standing to raise. It does not make you Doyne Farmer or Jim Simons in a year. It makes you the kind of beginner who is now allowed in the room where those people work, and puts you on the only path that reaches them: compounding artifacts, not accumulating knowledge.

The Mastery Pyramid

The pyramid is load-bearing from the bottom up. You cannot do real alpha research from a dysregulated nervous system, and you cannot raise capital without an edge to raise against. Each layer is necessary for the one above it; the apex exists only when all four lower layers hold simultaneously. Two capabilities – AI leverage and a publishing cadence – run as vertical spines through every layer, because they are not stages you pass but disciplines you sustain.

Why the order is not negotiable

  • State sits at the base because every layer above it is a sequence of decisions, and decision quality is bounded by nervous-system regulation. Your own finding – that shame, not rumination, is your stress signature, and that it is somatic and action-oriented – means your base layer is trainable through movement and practice, not analysis. This is an advantage; treat it as infrastructure.
  • Foundations before craft because alpha research done without tooling fluency produces unfalsifiable stories, and done without domain theory produces overfit noise. You need both the machine and the worldview before the craft means anything.
  • Craft before leverage because capital raised against an unproven edge is the fastest way to destroy a reputation – the one asset in this business that does not regenerate. Dave's instinct here (start private, paper-trade first, maintain mystique) is correct risk management of your reputation, not just his.
  • Integration is emergent, not additive. The apex is not "do all four layers." It is the state where a complexity-economics insight becomes a DRL feature becomes a deployed signal becomes a research memo becomes an LP conversation – the same idea earning its keep four times. That is compounding, and it is the only defense against the dilettante's curse.
Part I · The Model – 03

The Belt System & Why a Year

A traditional progression is not arbitrary color. It is a sequence of prerequisites – each belt is the demonstrated mastery of the material that makes the next belt learnable. You cannot skip green to reach brown, because brown's techniques assume green's reflexes are already automatic. This is exactly how compounding works in research: walk-forward rigor assumes backtest hygiene; capital formation assumes a track record exists to form capital around.

The colored belts are the year. The dan grades are the decade. Below is the honest mapping – eight gradings across twelve months – anchored to the external forcing functions you already operate against, so that your real-world milestones are the gradings. The Oxford paper is your purple-belt test. The twelve-month track-record close is your black-belt test. Nothing is invented; the calendar is your sensei.

On compression

A real martial black belt takes three to five years. You are compressing to one. That is possible only because three things are already true that aren't true for a beginner: you bring a high math ceiling, a multi-year live options track record, and shipping engineering capability. Where you are actually a beginner – capital formation, risk formalization, DRL theory – the belts will feel slow and earned. Respect that. Skipped fundamentals are the only thing that breaks a black belt later.

Operating State – Body, Mind & Soul

Mastery is a trained body and a regulated nervous system that together hold decision quality through drawdown, rejection, and regime change.

This is the base of the pyramid because it is the substrate on which every other computation runs – and the substrate is not only mental. The mind runs on hardware. Cognitive clarity, stress tolerance, sustained energy, and the physiological calm that holds composure through a loss are all downstream of the body: cardiovascular capacity, strength, sleep, recovery. The contemplative and the athletic are not two practices competing for your morning; they are the two halves of Layer 0. Your own model – Feel – Surrender – Observe – Extract – Reprogram – is the mental closed loop; your training split is the physical one. You have an unusual advantage: you process through articulation and movement, not rumination, and your stressor is shame (somatic, action-oriented) rather than depressive collapse. That means your regulation tools are physical and procedural – exactly the tools that survive market stress. Neglect the body and the whole pyramid is built on a base that fatigues.

Layer 2 · Core Craft

Alpha Research & Strategy Derivation

Mastery is generating edge as a repeatable scientific process, and defending it against your own desire for it to be real.

This is the central craft – the thing the other disciplines exist to serve. Alpha research is the disciplined search for and validation of edge, and its hardest skill is adversarial honesty: the market pays you precisely to the extent that your edge survives every attempt you make to disprove it. Most strategies that "work" are overfit ghosts. The master treats every promising backtest as guilty until proven innocent – multiple-testing corrections, out-of-sample holdouts, economic-rationale gates ("why does this edge exist and who is paying for it?"). Dave's own instinct – maintain information asymmetry, the strategy is simple but the discipline is the moat – is the founder's version of this truth.

The discipline that defines the belt

Keep a research log in which you record every hypothesis, its economic rationale, its test, and its verdict – including the kills. A log with no dead hypotheses is a confession that you are confirming, not researching. The ratio of killed-to-kept is a direct readout of your alpha-research belt.

AI Leverage & Staying Current

Mastery is treating your toolset as a depreciating asset – and refreshing it faster than the field re-credentials around you.

This is not a chapter you finish; it is the spine running through all five layers, because the half-life of any specific tool is now measured in months. You already operate code-natively (the Dave transcript – building slides, research, and the whole "operating system" inside Armstrong through prompting – is exactly this). The danger is twofold: (1) staleness, mistaking last year's workflow for current best practice; (2) the inverse – chasing every new tool and never compounding. Mastery is a protocol that refreshes the toolset on a cadence while protecting the artifacts underneath from churn.

The current-ness rule

Because the field moves, this primer's tool names will age – the protocol will not.

When you reach for a specific model, framework, or library, verify it against the live state of the field rather than memory. The discipline is not "know the newest tool"; it is "have a fixed cadence for finding out, and a fixed benchmark for deciding whether it earns a place in the stack." Stack churn without a benchmark is just a new way to be a dilettante.

Part III · The Year

The Twelve-Month Belt Progression

This is the periodized plan: each belt is a training block with a focus, a curriculum slice across the disciplines, a drill set, and a single grading – the artifact that earns the rank. The gradings are deliberately your real forcing functions, so that passing the belt and hitting the milestone are the same act. Daily structure stays constant underneath: backtest/research as the spine (5–6 hrs), the protected 2-hr DRL block, 30+15 min of practice and synthesis, and the two daily asks.

白 · White – Foundations & Instrumentation · June, wks 1–4

Focus: build the dōjō before you train in it. Reproducible environment, clean point-in-time data, Thesis Engine logging your study and morning state. Disciplines: Tooling (white), State (white), AI-spine (white). Grading: a reproducible research repo a stranger could clone and run, plus 28 consecutive days of practice logs. Risk it removes: irreproducible results – the silent killer of every later claim.

Removes: the "unverified claim" risk; performance becomes an artifact.

橙 · Orange – Validation Rigor & DRL I · August, wks 11–18

Focus: prove the backtest isn't a ghost – walk-forward, purged/combinatorial CV, deflated Sharpe, the explicit economic rationale for the edge. First offline-RL experiment, honestly assessed (you already expect it to fail on data grounds – document why rigorously). Disciplines: Alpha (brown), DRL (green), Tooling (brown). Grading: walk-forward tearsheet + the RL writeup (a public GitHub artifact). Removes: overfitting risk.

On the shape of the year

Notice the arc: the first half (White–Green) is almost entirely foundations and craft – building the machine and proving the edge. The second half (Blue–Black) is leverage and integration – turning the proven edge into a firm. This is the pyramid, walked from base to apex over twelve months. You do not raise in month two because there is nothing yet to raise against; you do not still be polishing tooling in month ten because by then the machine must already run while you sell.

Part IV · Assessment & Compounding – 14

The Grading Standard

A belt is never awarded for feeling ready. It is awarded for performing the required demonstration in front of a standard you cannot argue with. The whole point of the artifact-per-belt design is that your gradings are self-verifying – a reproducible repo either reproduces or it doesn't; a wired check either cleared or it didn't; a drawdown either broke your rules or it didn't. This is the dilettante-proofing. You can lie to yourself about how much you know. You cannot lie to yourself about whether the artifact exists.

The honest failure mode

If a belt's artifact slips, you do not get to award yourself the next belt anyway. Slippage is information: it means a prerequisite isn't automatic yet. The correct response is to hold rank and re-drill – not to skip ahead and discover three belts later that the foundation was hollow. In martial terms: a black belt who never truly earned green has a beautiful belt and breakable knees.

Part IV · Assessment & Compounding – 15

The Compounding Engine

The apex of the pyramid is not a ninth skill. It is the state in which the eight disciplines stop being separate and start feeding each other – where the same intellectual move earns its keep in four places at once. This is the structural answer to the dilettante's curse: breadth becomes an asset rather than a liability only when the practices share a substrate and compound into common artifacts.

How one idea pays four times

Take the Intelligence-Inversion thesis. As complexity economics, it is the spine of the Oxford paper. As alpha research, it generates a regime hypothesis about hyperscaler valuation that you can test. As DRL, the regime signal becomes a feature or a reward-shaping term in a policy. As risk, the regime diagnostic tells the book when to de-risk. As network/capital, the published paper makes you the person an LP wants managing money through the regime change. One thesis, five disciplines, and a single widening track record carrying all of it. That is compounding – and it is invisible to anyone trying to copy a single one of your skills in isolation.

The artifact flywheel

Your publishing cadence is the flywheel that converts private practice into public reputation, on a schedule that forces the compounding:

  • Months 1/4/7/10 – complexity-economics essays (Oxford-directed).
  • Months 2/5/8/11 – Armstrong research memos (the alpha craft, made legible).
  • Months 3/6/9/12 – DRL implementation writeups on GitHub.

Twelve artifacts a year. Each one is simultaneously a grading (it proves a discipline), a network act (it earns access), and a piece of the track record (it compounds your reputation). Miss the cadence and the disciplines drift apart again into hobbies. Hold it and they fuse.

The five identities, one spine

Your identity architecture – Hedge Fund Manager, Complexity Economist, Athlete, Mahamudra Meditator, Traveler, grounded in Knowledge / Aesthetic Experience / Love – is not five lives competing for hours. It is the pyramid described from the inside. The Athlete and the Meditator are Layer 0, the operating state on which everything computes. The Economist and the Fund Manager are Layers 1–3. The Traveler is the aesthetic-experience input that keeps the whole system from collapsing into pure achievement – and the eventual site of the contemplative-operator resolution. The integration is the point. Mastery is when these stop trading off and start subsidizing one another.

The Dan Arc

Black belt is the end of the book and the beginning of the art. The year gets you to shodan: a sound, independent operator with a complete track record and the standing to raise. What follows is the multi-year arc where competence becomes mastery and mastery becomes something quieter. The dan grades are not earned on a calendar; they are earned on compounding.

The deepest grading of all

The highest dan is not more alpha. It is non-reactive capacity sustained over a career.

You have already identified the real long-horizon question: that Mahamudra practice tends, over time, to reduce attachment to external achievement, while fund management compounds on exactly that achievement. The naïve reading treats this as a contradiction to be managed. The master's reading is that it is the point – that the rarest operator is the one who can run a performing book from a place of genuine non-attachment, because that is the only psychology that holds its decision quality through every regime, every drawdown, every rejection. The composure that earns your black belt and the equanimity your practice cultivates are the same capacity, met from two directions. When they fully meet, that is godan. Everything in this book is, ultimately, in service of arriving there with a track record in hand.

You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems – and then, if you have built them well, you keep your composure while they compound.
