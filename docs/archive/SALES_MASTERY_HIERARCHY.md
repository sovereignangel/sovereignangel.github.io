## PART 3: THE TRUST-BUILDING HIERARCHY

**Bridgewater Principle:** Trust is earned through demonstrated competence + repeated follow-through.

### Stage 1: Introduction (Week 1)

**Goal:** They know who you are and understand your value prop in 60 seconds.

**Mechanism:**
- Warm intro from mutual connection (1-2 sentences: who you are, why you matter, why intro matters)
- Example: "Lori builds options analytics tools for traders and manages a deep tech fund. She spotted the gap between what traders need and what exists. She should talk to you about [specific reason]."

**Your Job:**
- Respond to intro within 4 hours
- Add context: "Thanks for intro. Here's what I'm working on: [1 paragraph]. I'd love your thoughts on [specific question]."
- Suggest 3 time slots for next week
- Don't oversell

**KPI:** Response rate to intro within 24 hours: 100%

---

### Stage 2: First Conversation (Week 1-2)

**Goal:** Demonstrate you're credible and you understand their world.

**Mechanism:**
- 30-min call or coffee
- YOU ask questions first (60% listening, 40% talking)
- Come prepared: know their background, recent move, what they care about

**Your Job:**
- Ask one thing they're solving for that's hard
- Listen without interrupting
- Share ONE insight that's relevant to them (not about you)
- If appropriate, offer value: intro to someone, article, data
- Close with: "Can I stay in touch? I'd love to [specific small thing: send you ideas, intro you to X]"

**Do NOT:**
- Pitch your fund in first call
- Ask for money/investment in first call
- Talk about yourself for >10 minutes
- Use sales language ("I'd love to partner with you")

**KPI:** They say "yes, stay in touch" → You have permission to maintain relationship

---

### Stage 3: Value Delivery (Week 2-4)

**Goal:** Show that you follow through and think about them.

**Mechanism:**
- Send ONE thoughtful thing within 2 weeks of first call
- Options:
  - Intro to someone they should know (with context why)
  - Article/research relevant to their problem
  - Feedback on their idea (if you offered)
  - Invite to event/dinner where they'll meet someone useful

**Your Job:**
- Make the intro/value immediate and specific
- One sentence: "Thought of you when reading this—the finding about [X] aligns with what you mentioned."
- Don't ask for anything in return yet

**KPI:** Delivered value, they acknowledge it positively

---

### Stage 4: Repeated Touch (Month 1-3)

**Goal:** Top of mind (not annoying).

**Mechanism:**
- Monthly cadence: A check-in, a piece of value, OR an invite
- Rotate types: "Saw this and thought of you" → Invite to thing → Intro to useful person
- Use: Email, DM, tweet engagement (like/retweet their stuff)

**Your Job:**
- 2-3 min investment per touch (not labor-intensive)
- Genuine relevance (don't send random stuff)
- Occasional longer meeting (quarterly coffee/dinner for Tier 1)

**KPI:** Response rate to emails: 50%+. They initiate occasionally: 1-2x per year

---

### Stage 5: Mutual Advocacy (Month 3+)

**Goal:** They know what you do and recommend you to others.

**Mechanism:**
- They've now seen your work (you've shipped, they've watched)
- They understand your thesis
- They've referred you or been helped by an intro you made
- When someone asks "Should I work with Lori?" they say yes

**Your Job:**
- Ask if you can stay in touch monthly (formalizes the relationship)
- Share wins (new customer, feature shipped, fund progress) in a lightweight way (Twitter, monthly update)
- Invite to events where your peer cohort gathers
- One ask per year (when appropriate): intro, capital, advice

**KPI:** They introduce you to someone: 1+ per year. NPS for relationship: 8+/10

---

### Stage 6: Compounding Partnership (Year 2+)

**Goal:** You're a go-to person for each other. Repeated mutual benefit.

**Mechanism:**
- Regular cadence (quarterly minimum)
- You're involved in their deals/projects
- They're involved in yours
- You advocate for each other publicly

**Your Job:**
- Deliver material value, not just relationship maintenance
- Co-create when possible (joint research, co-invest, shared customers)
- Make introductions that matter

**KPI:** Multiple interactions per quarter. Mutual value created. They have credibility to vouch for you to tier-1 people

## PART 4: BRIDGEWATER AUDIT FRAMEWORK

### The Five Key Questions (for each stage)

**For every relationship + ask, audit against these:**

#### Q1: Am I Clear?
- They know what I do in <30 seconds (one-liner test)
- They know what I want/what problem I solve
- **KPI:** "Can you explain to me what Lori does?" → Accurate answer in 1-2 sentences

#### Q2: Is This Real?
- Am I solving a genuine problem they have?
- Are they genuinely interested (not being polite)?
- **Red Line:** If they say "interesting" 3x but don't commit to next step → Not real interest
- **KPI:** They volunteer a follow-up action / meeting without me asking

#### Q3: Am I Trustworthy?
- Have I done what I said I'd do? (follow-ups, intros, timeline)
- Do I know my stuff? (they sense credibility)
- Do I care about their interests or just mine?
- **KPI:** They tell someone else about me (warm intro rate = trust signal)

#### Q4: Is This the Right Time?
- Is their pain acute now (not theoretical)?
- Do they have budget/authority to say yes?
- Are they in decision mode or learning mode?
- **Red Line:** Asking for fund investment from someone in "learning mode"

#### Q5: What's My Real Competitive Advantage?
- Why me vs. other options?
- Can I articulate this clearly without BS?
- **KPI:** They couldn't easily replace me with someone else (you have a unique combination)

---

### The Three Ruin Conditions

**If any of these are true, audit and fix immediately:**

#### Ruin 1: Fragmented Network (All 30 Are Weak)
- No one knows you deeply
- All relationships are 6-month dormant
- No warm intros happening
- **Signal:** Inbound = 0/month. All meetings from you asking.
- **Fix:** Pick 10 from top 30. Touch each in next 2 weeks. Share something real.

#### Ruin 2: Message Unclear (People Don't "Get It")
- Same ask, wildly different understanding of what you do
- "Oh, I thought you were doing [totally wrong thing]"
- Your story changes depending on audience
- **Signal:** Conversion rates vary 2x by audience
- **Fix:** Write one-liner. Test on 10 strangers. Refine.

#### Ruin 3: No Value Delivery (All Takers, No Givers)
- Every interaction is you asking
- You haven't made a meaningful intro in 6 months
- People avoid your DMs
- **Signal:** Response rates <20%. They delay. They ghost.
- **Fix:** Spend month being useful (intros, insights, no asks). Reset.

## PART 10: RED FLAGS & COURSE CORRECTIONS

If you see these signals, STOP and audit immediately:

### Red Flag 1: Response Rate Crashes
- Was 15%, now 8%
- **Cause:** Message unclear, ICP wrong, or timing off
- **Fix:** A/B test message. Redefine ICP. Try new channel.

### Red Flag 2: All Meetings From You Asking
- <20% warm intros
- **Cause:** Network doesn't understand what you do, or trust is low
- **Fix:** Focus month on value delivery (intros, insights, no asks)

### Red Flag 3: Top Relationships Dormant
- Tier 1 contact last touched 60+ days ago
- **Cause:** You're focused on growth, forgetting relationships
- **Fix:** Block 30 min/day for top 10. Non-negotiable.

### Red Flag 4: Can't Articulate One-Liner Clearly
- Different explanation every time you're asked
- **Cause:** Thesis unclear or you're not being honest about your edge
- **Fix:** Write it. Test on 10 people. Refine daily.

### Red Flag 5: No Value Flowing (All Takers, No Givers)
- Last 5 interactions: You asked, they said no or stalled
- **Cause:** Credibility gap or bad timing
- **Fix:** Month of pure value delivery (zero asks)
